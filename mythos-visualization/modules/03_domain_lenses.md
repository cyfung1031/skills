# Module 03 — Domain Lens Pack

Load this module for domain-specific checklists or examples.

## UI/Product

Identify app/page/state, intended task, primary controls, hierarchy, reading order, visible states, overlays, truncation, clipping, density, legibility, and visible accessibility risks. Screenshot-only UI cannot verify DOM, CSS cascade, JS, backend, keyboard, screen reader, analytics, or responsiveness.

## Chart/Data

Identify chart type, marks, encodings, axes, labels, legends, scales, units, filters, uncertainty cues, and interaction hints. Distinguish exact values from pattern-only reads. Avoid significance, causality, sample-size, or missing-data claims unless source-backed.

## Map/Spatial

Identify extent, basemap, route/region/layer, labels, symbols, scale/projection clues, and approximate spatial relationships. Do not claim geospatial accuracy, legal boundary, route optimality, distance, current traffic, or live conditions unless verified.

## Diagram/System

Identify entities, connectors, direction, grouping, labels, interfaces, layers, and ambiguity between visual layout and real system behavior. For technical drawings, distinguish visible schematic relation from implementation reality.

## Photo/Scene/Object/Creature

Identify scene type, primary subjects, parts, pose, occlusion, lighting, background, classification confidence, lookalikes, and safety/identity limits. Prefer broad category when exact identity is unsupported.

## Artwork/Illustration

Separate subject matter from interpretation. Describe composition, motifs, style, mood, symbolism, and ambiguity. Avoid claims about artist intent, provenance, or exact meaning unless provided.

## Meme/Social

Identify format, caption-image relation, visible references, likely joke mechanics, audience assumptions, and missing cultural context. Avoid asserting private intent or universal interpretation.

## Abstract/Formal

Focus on color, shape, rhythm, texture, figure/ground, mood, and possible resemblance. Avoid over-classifying representational content.

## Scientific/Industrial/Medical-looking imagery

Describe visible structures, acquisition-like appearance, measurement limits, artifacts, and uncertainty. Do not diagnose, certify safety, or recommend operations from appearance alone.

## Motion/Video

Separate frame observations from temporal claims. Claim motion/timing only after multiple frames, video metadata, logs, or runtime inspection.
